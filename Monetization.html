Types of Monetization

Here are a few ways companies try to make money from people who play video games

Loot boxes

The best way to describe loot boxes is to compare them to a mystery bag. Basically, you are paying money for a set of randomly selected items. The odds are decided by the developers and/or publishers of the game. You have zero control over what you get and the rarities and effects will vary. Items that are in the boxes have ranged from cosmetics that donâ€™t affect gameplay to power ups that can potentially put you ahead of others. It differs from game to game. Overwatch was the primary example of this. You had the option to pay money to open a box with 4 random skins in them. There is an option to earn loot boxes just buy playing the game. Keep in mind that customers have pay $60 to own and play Overwatch, so for Blizzard, the loot boxes are the icing to the money cake.
